# Business Insights & Recommendations

## 1. Overall Customer Churn

### Insight
The overall customer churn rate is 52%, indicating that more than half of the customer base is at risk of leaving.

### Business Impact
High customer churn may significantly reduce long-term revenue and customer lifetime value.

### Recommendation
The bank should prioritize customer retention strategies and improve engagement for high-risk customer segments.

---

## 2. Province-Wise Churn Analysis

### Insight
British Columbia (BC) shows the highest churn rate at 56.86%, compared to other provinces.

### Business Impact
Higher regional churn may negatively impact customer growth and revenue stability in BC.

### Recommendation
Develop province-specific retention campaigns and loyalty programs targeting BC customers.

---

## 3. High-Value Customers

### Insight
A small group of customers contributes disproportionately high transaction value.

### Business Impact
The bank is highly dependent on top-value customers for revenue generation.

### Recommendation
Introduce premium banking services and personalized offers for high-value customers.

---

## 4. Province Revenue Analysis

### Insight
Quebec (QC) generates the highest transaction revenue among all provinces.

### Business Impact
QC represents a strategically important revenue-generating region.

### Recommendation
Increase customer engagement and marketing investment in high-performing provinces.

---

## 5. Campaign Performance

### Insight
Campaign CTR is above 50%, indicating strong customer engagement with campaigns.

### Business Impact
Marketing campaigns are successfully attracting customer interaction.

### Recommendation
Focus future optimization on improving conversion quality and customer retention.

## 6. Conversion Efficiency

### Insight
The conversion rate is approximately 49.87%, indicating efficient customer progression from clicks to conversions.

### Business Impact
Campaign funnel performance appears strong within the simulated environment.

Scale high-performing campaigns and monitor campaign acquisition costs.

## 8. Customer Inactivity Risk Analysis

### Business Question

Which customers show signs of disengagement?

### Evidence

Several customers have not transacted for extended periods.

Examples:

| Customer ID | Recency |
|------------|----------|
| 79 | 724 Days |
| 74 | 664 Days |
| 50 | 662 Days |

### Insight

High recency values indicate prolonged inactivity and elevated churn risk.

### Recommendation

Launch re-engagement campaigns for customers inactive for more than 180 days.

Examples:

- Cashback offers
- Fee waivers
- Loyalty rewards

### Expected Impact

Reduce customer churn and reactivate dormant accounts.
